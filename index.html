Economics

Electric cars are less expensive to run than comparable internal combustion engine cars, due to the lower cost of maintenance and energy.

The average operating cost of an electric vehicle per year is three time less than internal combustion cars.

Environmental aspects

Electric cars have significant reduction of local air pollution over the internal combustion engine cars, as they do not directly emit pollutants.

Reduce dependence of petroleum, and also reduce CO2 and other greenhouse gas emissions.

Performance

Electric motors provide high power-to-weight ratios, and have flat torque curve down to zero speed.

High acceleration due to reduced drivetrain friction losses, and can also use a direct motor-to-wheel configuration which increases the available power.

Energy efficiency

Electric motors are more efficient than internal combustion engines in converting stored energy into driving a vehicle, up to 4 time.

Regenerative braking can recover as much as one fifth of the energy normally lost during braking.

Powerful lithium-based batteries

Lithium-based batteries are used for their high power and energy density, giving outstanding range to all our models.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long electric cars last?

By default, electric cars last longer than fossil cars. Less moving parts and as a whole, less strain on components.

How fast can electric cars go?

Electric cars can go very fast. The instant torque provided by the electric motors can send them from 0 to 60mph (0-100km/h) in under 3 seconds.

How long electric cars take to charge?

This depends on a few factors. There are 3 levels of charging, from a 110, 220, or 440 volt system. And also, how big is your battery pack.

Is home charger include in package with car?

Yes. All our modles have home charger included in offer.

How much cost to charge electric car at charging station?

90% or more of level 2 chargers are absolutely free. Level 3 chargers can sometimes be free, but often cost at least a few dollars (6$-10$ on average).

Why don't electric cars have solar panels?

The simple answer to that is it would take FAR TOO LONG to fully charge to 100%. We are talking about days if not weeks to get a full charge.
